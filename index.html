Sima Kavand, MD

Board-Certified Internal Medicine Physician | Dermatologist

Dr. Sima Kavand earned her medical degree at Tehran University of Medical Sciences. Before relocating to the United States, she completed a four-year dermatology residency and was a board certified dermatologist who practiced for 5 years.

In the US, she first worked as a postdoctoral research fellow in the Department of Dermatology at the University of Minnesota. Her research findings have been published in several reputable dermatology journals. She then completed her internal medicine residency at the Saint Francis Hospital affiliated with the University of Illinois.

Dr. Kavand leverages her extensive dermatology experience to concentrate on skin diseases within her primary care practice. She is interested in preventive medicine, especially screening for skin cancers and caring for patients with skin conditions such as acne, eczema, psoriasis, warts and any acute skin conditions concerns. She consistently enhances her knowledge through conference participation, workshops, and collaborating with colleagues.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How quickly can I schedule an appointment?

    Scheduling your appointment is easy and immediate. Prevention and early treatment can be crucial to skin health. That is why we offer same-day and next-day appointments for urgent situations. Even if not urgent, please let us know how we can work around your schedule – we will not let our schedule force you to skip a skin exam or other preventative care.

  • How often should I get screened for skin cancer?

    Ideally annually; however, those who have a family history of skin cancer or other risk factors may want to talk with us about coming in more than once a year for a skin cancer screening.

  • Are Injectable Neurotoxins and fillers safe?

    Injectable Neurotoxins have been extensively researched for 30 years and are found to be extremely safe when handled by well-trained providers.

  • Should I come to the office with the least makeup if I have an upcoming procedure?

    If you are having a procedure, please come with the least amount of makeup/product on your face.
